2001 BMW 740iL (E38) – Engine & Transmission Swap

Full Mechanical Restoration · Winter 2021 – Present

Engine Swap Transmission Rebuild BMW INPA Cam Timing Correction

Overview

Purchased as a running vehicle with heavy exhaust smoke, this V8-powered luxury sedan became a full mechanical restoration. The project involved diagnosing complex multi-system failures — including a mistimed replacement engine and a failed transmission install — and resolving each through systematic root cause analysis and hands-on repair.

Photos

Major Work Performed

  • Initial diagnostics and cooling system repair (coolant pipe replacement)
  • PCV system service and oil system evaluation
  • Full engine removal and replacement with sourced M62 unit
  • Transmission replacement after improper torque converter installation
  • BMW INPA diagnostics to isolate misfires to one cylinder bank
  • Camshaft timing correction using timing kit and TDC alignment procedure
  • Restored proper firing across all cylinders after timing correction
  • Identified and replaced cracked transmission valve body using known-good unit to resolve rough shifting
  • Catalytic converter and muffler modification for improved exhaust flow
  • Front bumper sourcing and installation with passenger-side mount straightening
  • Driver-side fender panel sourcing and installation
  • H&R lowering spring installation

2004 Lexus GX470 – Frame Repair & Structural Restoration

Structural Fabrication & Rust Remediation · Summer 2024 – Summer 2025

Structural Welding Metal Fabrication Rust Remediation Chassis Repair

Overview

Successfully restored the structural integrity of a Lexus GX470 by remediating extensive frame corrosion. This project involved high-precision metal fabrication, structural welding, and chemical treatment to halt oxidation and ensure long-term safety and durability of the load-bearing chassis structure.

Work Performed

  • Rust removal using pneumatic needle scaler on accessible frame areas
  • Cut out severely corroded sections and fabricated custom metal replacement panels
  • Repaired and precisely realigned broken rear trailing arm mount
  • Applied phosphoric acid solution to convert remaining rust into a stable surface, followed by protective paint
  • Replaced corroded brake hard lines from under driver's seat to rear calipers
  • Replaced rear sway bar links including one fully rusted-off link

Skills Demonstrated

Structural Welding Custom Metal Fabrication Rust Remediation Suspension Mount Alignment Brake Line Replacement Chemical Surface Treatment

2006 Range Rover Supercharged – Diagnostic Recovery & Restoration

$500 Acquisition · Fall 2025 – Present

$500 Acquisition 450–600% Projected ROI Electrical Diagnostics Paint Prep

Overview

Acquired a non-starting luxury SUV for $500 through systematic identification of a repairable electronic fault. By bypassing a failed transmission inhibitor circuit, full drivetrain functionality was restored. The project now focuses on high-end aesthetic restoration and paint correction to maximize resale value.

Work Performed

  • Identified and bypassed a faulty transmission inhibitor circuit (Neutral Safety Switch) to restore starting capability
  • Conducted drivetrain validation on the 4.2L Supercharged V8 — verified shift quality and thermal management
  • Complete headlight remanufacture: housing separation, new high-clarity lenses, butyl seal for moisture protection
  • Sourced and installed replacement interior and exterior trim pieces
  • Multi-stage surface preparation including high-grit sanding and failed clear-coat leveling on hood and roof
